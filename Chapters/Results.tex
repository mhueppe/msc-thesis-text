\chapter{Results} \label{chapter:results} 
The results presented in this chapter provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed model across multiple perspectives. First, in \autoref{sec:results/evaluation} the predictive performance is assessed on both the \gls{pdb} and \gls{crims} datasets, highlighting differences in generalization and practical applicability. Subsequently, the influence of model design choices is examined through a systematic hyperparameter optimization analysis in \autoref{sec:results/hyperparameter_tuning}. Finally, \autoref{sec:results/interpretation} further investigates the learned representations using interpretability analyses, including cluster similarity effects and feature ablations, to better understand the factors driving model performance. Performances between training and validation or test set are all reported with the smallest similarity of 30\% between the two to ensure confidence towards generalization of the findings (cf. \autoref{fig:cluster_similarity_performance}).
